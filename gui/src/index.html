User Guide

What is ZECK?

ZECK recovers your Zcash from a ZecWallet Lite seed phrase and sweeps it to a modern wallet like Zashi or YWallet. You need your 24-word BIP-39 seed phrase and the Unified Address of your destination wallet. Nothing else.

Before you start

  • Your 24-word seed phrase from ZecWallet Lite.
  • A destination Unified Address (starts with u1…) from a modern wallet such as Zashi or YWallet. Transparent (t1…) and Sapling (zs…) addresses are not accepted.
  • A stable internet connection. Long scans can reconnect automatically if the server drops — but don't put your machine to sleep mid-scan.

Step 1 — Welcome

The welcome screen explains what ZECK does. If you have a .dat file rather than a seed phrase, follow the link to Zexcavator instead. Otherwise click I have my 24-word seed phrase.

Step 2 — Seed Entry

Paste your full 24-word seed phrase (separated by spaces). Click Validate seed or press Enter — ZECK checks the BIP-39 checksum locally. The phrase is blurred by default; toggle Show words on screen if you need to proofread it. Your seed is never transmitted anywhere.

Step 3 — Configure

  • Network: Use Mainnet unless you were testing with play money.
  • Wallet birthday: The block number when your wallet was created. An earlier birthday is safe but means a longer scan. Use Estimate from date to convert a calendar date, or Auto-detect to probe the blockchain automatically.
  • Accounts to scan: Most users had just one account. Leaving this at 20 is safe for nearly everyone.
  • Destination address: Paste your u1… Unified Address here. Click Validate destination to confirm it has an Orchard or Sapling receiver.
  • Server: Leave on Recommended unless you run your own lightwalletd node.

When you're happy with the settings, click Start Scan.

Step 4 — Scan

ZECK first probes transparent UTXOs (results appear within seconds), then syncs all shielded compact blocks. Progress, phase, and estimated time remaining are shown in real time. Funded accounts appear in the table as they're discovered.

Scans can take anywhere from a few minutes (recent birthday) to several hours (birthday near Sapling activation in 2018). The ETA warms up after about 15 seconds.

You can cancel and resume at any time. Re-run ZECK with the same seed, network, birthday, and account settings to pick up from the last saved block.

Step 5 — Sweep

Review the proposed transactions before anything is broadcast. The table shows each account, step (Shield or Sweep), destination, gross amount, fee, and net received. Transparent funds are shielded first in a separate transaction.

Check the irreversible acknowledgement box, then click Sweep Funds. Transactions are broadcast to the Zcash network — this cannot be undone.

Step 6 — Complete

ZECK shows the txid for each transaction. Save a recovery report to a text file for your records.

Troubleshooting

  • Seed validation fails: Check for typos, extra spaces, or missing words. All 24 words must be valid BIP-39 words in the correct order.
  • Auto-detect finds no birthday: Your wallet may have no transparent history. Use the date picker to estimate the birthday manually instead.
  • Scan seems stuck: Long pauses during the shielded sync are normal — the server may be catching up. Watch the block counter; as long as it's moving, the scan is working.
  • Connection drops: ZECK reconnects automatically up to 10 times. If it keeps failing, try switching the server preset to use a different public node.
  • No funds found: Confirm you used the correct network (Mainnet vs Testnet) and that the birthday is early enough to cover when you first received ZEC.
  • Sweep fails: The workspace may be out of date. Go back to config and re-run the scan with the same settings to refresh it, then try sweeping again.

Frequently asked questions

  • Is my seed phrase safe? Yes. ZECK never transmits your seed. All key derivation happens locally. Only compact block data is downloaded from lightwalletd servers.
  • Can I use a transparent or Sapling address as my destination? No. ZECK requires a Unified Address with an Orchard or Sapling receiver (starts with u1). Get one from Zashi or YWallet.
  • What if I had more than one ZecWallet Lite account? ZECK scans up to 500 accounts automatically. If you enabled the gap-limit scan mode, it stops after 20 consecutive empty accounts by default — enough for almost all wallets.
  • What happens to my data after ZECK finishes? The local wallet workspace (compact block cache and derived keys) stays in the data directory you chose. You can delete it once you've confirmed your funds arrived safely.
  • Can I run ZECK offline? The scan phase requires a lightwalletd connection to download block data. Seed validation and key derivation are fully offline.
